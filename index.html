Background

2019 - 2021

Graduate degree

I completed the Master of Biomedical Engineering program at the University of Melbourne

2021 - 2022

Deep dive into assistive technology

As part of a technical team in Swinburne University, I developed an accessible and brand new assistive tech research lab. The goal of this project was to involve more people with accessibility needs to co-design novel assistive devices together. This is based on a saying by James Charlton, “Nothing about us, without us.”

I was also involved in a few product development projects, such as improving the design of portable ramps for wheelchair users, creating concepts for crutches with seats, and designing a sleek under-wheelchair bag for a client.

2022 - current

Current engineering role

I’m currently working as a biomedical engineer in Zyteq, an Australian assistive technology company specialising in speech-generating devices for clients with motor neurone disease (MND), cerebral palsy, and other conditions.
